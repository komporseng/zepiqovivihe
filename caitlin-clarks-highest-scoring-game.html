Caitlin Clark's Highest Scoring Game

 Posted on June 1, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  529 words  |  Aldo Pusey

Caitlin Clark, the standout senior guard for the Iowa Hawkeyes, has had an illustrious career, filled with record-breaking performances and exceptional scoring feats. Her highest scoring game came in the 2023-24 season, where she displayed her exceptional scoring prowess against Ohio State, showcasing her ability to dominate on the court with an outstanding performance.

The Record-Breaking Game

In the game against Ohio State on January 21, 2024, Clark delivered a truly remarkable performance, scoring a season-high 45 points in a closely contested match that ended in a 100-92 overtime loss for the Hawkeyes. Despite the defeat, Clark's scoring prowess was on full display as she shot 12-for-25, including 7-for-18 from 3-point range, while also contributing with three rebounds and seven assists. This game marked her 48th career game with 30 or more points, solidifying her place in NCAA basketball history as the player with the most 30-point games over the last 25 seasons.

Consistent Scoring Prowess

Clark's ability to consistently deliver high-scoring performances is evident from her scoring record throughout the season. Leading up to her 45-point game against Ohio State, she had already showcased her scoring prowess in various games, including:

  • 32 points against Wisconsin on January 16, 2024
  • 30 points against Indiana on January 13, 2024
  • 26 points against Purdue on January 10, 2024
  • 29 points against Rutgers on January 5, 2024
  • 40 points against Michigan State on January 2, 2024

This highlights her remarkable consistency and ability to deliver exceptional scoring performances throughout the season.

Impressive Milestones and Records

Clark's scoring abilities extend beyond individual games, with her career achievements positioning her among the all-time greats in women's college basketball. Notably, she became the fourth all-time leading scorer in women's college basketball with 3,351 career points, as of her game against Ohio State. Her remarkable milestone of scoring 20 or more points in 105 out of her 120 games with the Hawkeyes further emphasizes her consistent scoring prowess and impact on the court.

Furthermore, Clark's offensive capabilities are not limited to scoring alone. She has also demonstrated her playmaking skills by becoming the Big Ten's all-time assist leader and the only Division I player to have surpassed 3,000 points, 900 assists, and 800 rebounds.

Projected Records and Future Potential

Looking ahead, Clark is on pace to break the Division I women's scoring record, currently held by Kelsey Plum, and is projected to achieve this milestone as early as February 22, 2024, against Indiana. Her remarkable scoring average of 31 points per game further solidifies her potential to break this record sooner, potentially as early as February 15, 2024, against Michigan.

Furthermore, should Clark choose to return for a fifth year, she has the potential to shatter multiple records and leave an indelible mark on women's college basketball. Her scoring prowess and all-around impact on the game position her as a transcendent talent with the potential to redefine the record books and etch her name in NCAA basketball history.

Caitlin Clark's highest scoring game of 45 points against Ohio State in the 2023-24 season stands as a testament to her exceptional scoring prowess and her potential to break and set new records in women's college basketball.
