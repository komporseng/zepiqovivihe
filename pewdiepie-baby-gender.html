Fans Left Wondering after PewDiePie Reveals Baby Gender in Unfashioned Way

 Posted on July 18, 2024  |  12 minutes  |  2446 words  |  Aldo Pusey

Felix Arvid Ulf Kjellberg, widely recognized as PewDiePie, and his wife Marzia Kjellberg has recently revealed the gender of their soon-to-be-born baby in a truly spectacular and magical way.

On May 5, 2023, PewDiePie uploaded a video on YouTube titled, “Think it’s time we did a gender reveal,” where he hinted at the gender of their upcoming baby. In the video, the couple celebrated Koinobori, a Japanese holiday where families hoist fish-shaped flags to pray for their children’s prosperity and health. 

At the end of the video, the Youtuber showed the floating fish, a symbol of Koinobori and hinted at the gender of their child by saying, “If you know what these mean, then you know the gender.”

On the same day as the video uploaded by PewDiePie, Marzia shared an Instagram post featuring her husband alongside the caption “Hint hint.”

After analyzing various clues about PewDiePie and Marzia’s pregnancy, their fans have concluded that the couple is expecting a baby boy. 

In response to these clues, one fan expressed their excitement by stating that May 5th is recognized as Tango no Sekku, a public holiday in Japan that celebrates the health and happiness of male children. This information led the fan to conclude that the couple is likely having a baby boy.

Similarly, another fan added their perspective on the matter, stating, “Well the Koinobori hint confirms it, ITS A BOY. Carp streamers were originally flown as part of Japanese Boys Day with a carp representing each son for those unaware. What a beautiful way to reveal.”

However, not all fans could grasp the subtle hints dropped by PewDiePie and Marzia regarding the gender of their baby. One social media user made a lighthearted comment, jokingly suggesting that the couple might be having a “baby fish.”

Despite the widespread assumption among fans that PewDiePie and Marzia’s baby is a boy, the couple has not officially confirmed or denied this speculation. They have also not revealed the due date for the baby.

PewDiePie and his wife Marzia Kjellberg were rumored to have given birth to a black child. (Source: Twitter)

In addition to the recent speculation regarding the gender of their upcoming baby, there have been other rumors in the past. One such rumor was that the Youtuber and his wife had welcomed a black baby, which gained traction after the account TERMINALLY ONLINE posted a collage photo featuring the couple and a black baby on Twitter.

The rumor was considered surprising and unusual due to the couple’s different ethnic backgrounds. However, it was later revealed that the photo was a fake and a hoax. 

PewDiePie and Kjellberg just announced their pregnancy on February 5, 2023, via Instagram and YouTube. The couple shared a video documenting their journey from taking a pregnancy test and receiving a positive result to visiting a doctor for an ultrasound and capturing images of the growing fetus. 

The video also featured footage of Marzia’s pregnancy belly and the couple’s excitement about starting a family. Their announcement quickly garnered attention and excitement from their fans and followers on social media.

Teenage Rapper Lil Purk to Serve 20 Years in Prison for Atlantic Station Double Murder

Lil Purk has been jailed 20 years in prison.

Teenage Rapper Lil Purk, real name Antonio Jackson, has been jailed for 20 years in prison for a deadly shooting near Atlantic Station in November 2022.

Five of the six defendants, Lil Purk, Demetrius Hill, Toney Wilson, Deandre Forston, and DeRodney Russell, entered negotiated guilty pleas, their sentences ranging from 20 to 40 years. 

The rapper was sentenced to 20 years, with 10 years to be served in custody and 10 years on probation. However, other suspects were sentenced to 40 years in jail.

Hill faced a more severe sentence, having negotiated a guilty plea resulting in 40 years of imprisonment. Of this term, he is slated to spend 30 years in custody.

Wilson received a similar 40-year sentence but with a different distribution—20 years in custody and an equal period on probation.

Forston and Russell, like their co-defendants, entered negotiated guilty pleas and received matching sentences of 40 years. In their cases, 20 years are to be served in custody, followed by an additional 20 years on probation.

Two young boys, 12-year-old Zyion Charles, and 15-year-old Cameron Jackson were left dead, and four other individuals were injured.

Zyion’s mother, Deerica Charles, spoke emotionally before the Atlanta City Council Public Safety Committee, revealing her desperate attempts to divert her son from a dangerous path of criminal behavior.

Despite her pleas to law enforcement, citing her son was involved in car break-ins, a prevalent form of street crime in Atlanta, she received little assistance. 

Deerica said, “I tried, y’all. I called the police office almost 30 times in the last two years, and they told me they can’t do nothing. I said he’s out and breaking into these folks’ cars, can you please get him off the street.”

On the other hand, Cameron’s family remembered him as a teenager with a passion for science, animals, cooking, and dirt bike riding. He had also been practicing boxing since the age of nine. 

Cameron’s mother, Tiffany Smith said, “Cameron has been a boxer since 9-years-old, and he was heading for a professional boxing career. He trained five days a week with a pro boxing coach.”

Additionally, according to 11Alive News, the shootout occurred on the 17th Street Bridge originated from two groups initially escorted off the Atlantic Station property due to unruly behavior and curfew violations.

Atlanta Police later confirmed their belief that the shooting was gang-related. However, Zyion’s family denied his involvement in any gang activity.

Indie Artist Noelle Sucks Claims Olivia Rodrigo’s SNL Performance Is a Ripoff of Her Work

Noelle Sucks said, “I am pretty sure my art got stolen…”

Indie artist Noelle Sucks has voiced serious concerns over potential plagiarism, asserting that Olivia Rodrigo‘s recent Saturday Night Live (SNL) performance has striking similarities to her own previous work.

Sucks took to Instagram to share her perspective, stating, “I am pretty sure my art got stolen and put on Saturday Night Live for Olivia Rodrigo’s most recent SNL performance. I just want to know what you guys think after I show you some facts.”

The base of her argument lies in a side-by-side comparison of her music video called ‘YOUR MOM CALLS ME’ from a few years ago with Rodrigo’s SNL performance, both centered around a “tea party gone wrong” concept that transitions from calm and pretty to angsty.

Sucks meticulously pointed out resemblances, emphasizing identical dance moves, such as cake smashing, sugar pouring, and hand gestures, performed by both herself and Rodrigo and their respective dance crews.

Notably, she also underscored the similarities in camerawork between the two videos, suggesting a potential replication or inspiration.

Furthermore, Sucks realized both she and Rodrigo’s creative director, Tarik Mikou, have a mutual friend who had publicly shared Sucks’ music on social media.

Sucks’ friend commented, “@noellesucks!!! This guy stole the Your Mom Calls Me music video concept from u.”

Additionally, her other friends, suspecting plagiarism, commented on Mikou’s Instagram accusing him of wrongdoing, which was later swiftly deleted.

Upon messaging her concerns on Mikou’s Instagram, Sucks faced no reply. Moreover, Mikou unfollowed their mutual connection and remained unresponsive to Sucks’s direct message on Instagram.

Brief bio on Noelle Sucks

Noelle Sucks emerges as a distinctive force in the music scene, defying easy categorization with a unique blend of indie, shoegaze, pop, and rock that creates an emotional soundscape, as per her official website. 

Born into a musically talented family, Noelle’s artistic journey took a pivotal turn after a life-altering brush with death at the age of 13 during a major surgery. 

Raised with deeply spiritual views by her parents, the absence of any heavenly visions during this near-death experience challenged and reshaped her long-held beliefs, prompting a profound transformation in her perspective on life.

The recovery period from her surgery, though sidelining her from traditional schooling, became a catalyst for Sucks’ creativity. During this time, she began conceptualizing music videos for existing songs and discovered her passion for editing homemade videos. 

The newfound interest propelled her towards film school, and eventually, a return to music was sparked by a significant breakup at the age of 23, inspiring her to create her first song. 

Describing her early musical endeavors as a process of “catching up,” the artist filled in the gaps from the years she spent immersed in film and other pursuits.

Her musical career took a significant turn with the release of her ‘Out of Orbit’ EP in 2022. Her ability to traverse difficult emotions through her music was demonstrated in the EP, which delved into the complexities of emotions resulting from a breakup with her friend group. 

Notably, her connections nurtured at Emo Nite, an event central to her personal and artistic growth, have come full circle with her signing to Emo Nite’s new label, Graveboy Records. 

The debut single ‘Ugly Ugly,’ released on September 22, 2023, stands as a significant milestone.
