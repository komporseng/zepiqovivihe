Who owns the Dallas Cowboys?

 Posted on August 11, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  537 words  |  Reinaldo Massengill

The ownership structure of the Dallas Cowboys has been a topic of significant interest and discussion, given the team's prominence in the NFL.

Since purchasing the team in 1989, Jerry Jones has been the face of the Cowboys.

His role as the owner and general manager has played a pivotal part in shaping the team's success and failures over the years. 

Jerry Jones: The Face of the Dallas Cowboys

Jerry Jones, the owner and general manager of the Dallas Cowboys, has been a dominant figure in the franchise's operations since he acquired the team in 1989.

His leadership style and decision-making have left both success and failure at his feet, with the team experiencing both regular-season success and playoff disappointments.

Jones has been vocal about his commitment to the team's success, often making public statements about the team's direction and future plans.

Impact of Jerry Jones' Leadership Style

Former NFL veteran and three-time Super Bowl champion, Mark Schlereth, has been vocal about the impact of Jerry Jones' leadership style on the Cowboys' title drought.

Schlereth attributes the team's struggles in big moments to the organizational structure under Jones.

He emphasizes the lack of empowerment within the organization, leading to a culture of finger-pointing and a failure to take responsibility, ultimately hindering the team's success on the field.

Analysis of Recent Offseason and Player Perspectives

The recent offseason has seen significant roster attrition, with the Cowboys losing key players and facing challenges in strengthening the roster.

The team's approach, under Jones' leadership, has been a subject of scrutiny.

The messaging from the Cowboys' front office shifted from being "all in" on upgrading the roster to a more strategic and conservative approach, as reflected in their minimal spending in free agency.

Outlook

The ownership structure of the Dallas Cowboys, with Jerry Jones at the helm, has been a subject of analysis and scrutiny.

Jones' dual role as owner and general manager has shaped the team's trajectory, with both successes and challenges attributed to his leadership style.

The impact of organizational dynamics on player morale and team performance underscores the significance of effective leadership and management within the franchise.

The Dallas Cowboys' ownership structure, led by Jerry Jones, has been a defining aspect of the team's journey in the NFL. The interplay between ownership, management, and player dynamics continues to shape the team's narrative, contributing to a depth of analysis and public discourse on the Cowboys' trajectory in professional football.
