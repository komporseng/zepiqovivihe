The Walking Dead: Who is Dead and Who is Alive in the Final Season

 Posted on May 25, 2024  |  8 minutes  |  1532 words  |  Aldo Pusey

The Walking Dead, the hit zombie apocalypse series based on the comic book of the same name, has reached its final season after 11 years of thrilling and heartbreaking stories. The show has killed off many beloved characters along the way, leaving fans wondering who will survive the end of the world. In this article, we will recap who is dead and who is alive in the final season of The Walking Dead, and what their fates might be.

The Dead

The Walking Dead has never shied away from killing off major characters, often in brutal and shocking ways. Some of the most memorable deaths in the show include:

  • Rick Grimes (Andrew Lincoln): The protagonist of the show for the first nine seasons, Rick was presumed dead after he blew up a bridge to stop a horde of walkers from reaching his friends. However, it was later revealed that he was rescued by a mysterious helicopter and taken away to an unknown location. His fate remains a mystery, but he is expected to return in a spin-off movie trilogy.
  • Carl Grimes (Chandler Riggs): Rick’s son and one of the original survivors, Carl was bitten by a walker while saving a stranger named Siddiq. He died peacefully in his father’s arms, after writing letters to his loved ones and urging them to find a way to live together in peace.
  • Glenn Rhee (Steven Yeun): A former pizza delivery boy and Maggie’s husband, Glenn was one of the most loyal and optimistic members of the group. He met his end at the hands of Negan, the ruthless leader of the Saviors, who bashed his head in with a baseball bat named Lucille.
  • Abraham Ford (Michael Cudlitz): A former army sergeant and Rosita’s lover, Abraham was also killed by Negan in the same brutal scene as Glenn. He died defiantly, telling Negan to “suck my nuts”.
  • Beth Greene (Emily Kinney): Maggie’s younger sister and a sweet and innocent girl, Beth was shot in the head by Dawn, the leader of a group of corrupt cops at a hospital in Atlanta. She died in Daryl’s arms, who had grown close to her during their time together.
  • Hershel Greene (Scott Wilson): Maggie and Beth’s father and a former veterinarian, Hershel was a wise and compassionate man who acted as a moral compass for the group. He was decapitated by the Governor, the tyrannical leader of Woodbury, during an assault on the prison where the group was staying.
  • Lori Grimes (Sarah Wayne Callies): Rick’s wife and Carl’s mother, Lori died during childbirth, giving birth to Judith. She asked Maggie to perform a cesarean section on her, knowing that she would not survive. She said goodbye to Carl, who then shot her in the head to prevent her from turning into a walker.
  • Andrea (Laurie Holden): A former civil rights lawyer and one of the original survivors, Andrea was a strong and independent woman who often clashed with Rick. She was bitten by Milton, a scientist who worked for the Governor, and chose to kill herself before turning. She was found by Rick, Michonne, and Daryl, who had come to rescue her from Woodbury.
  • Shane Walsh (Jon Bernthal): Rick’s best friend and partner in the sheriff’s department, Shane became Rick’s rival and enemy after the apocalypse. He had an affair with Lori, believing that Rick was dead, and became obsessed with her and Carl. He tried to kill Rick twice, but was stabbed by him in self-defense. He then reanimated as a walker and was shot by Carl.
  • Dale Horvath (Jeffrey DeMunn): An elderly man and a former salesman, Dale was the owner of the RV that the group used as their home in the first two seasons. He was a kind and gentle soul who cared deeply for the group, especially Andrea. He was killed by a walker that Carl had accidentally freed from a swamp. Daryl mercy-killed him by shooting him in the head.
  • Merle Dixon (Michael Rooker): Daryl’s older brother and a former drug dealer, Merle was a violent and racist man who caused trouble for the group. He was left behind on a rooftop in Atlanta, where he cut off his own hand to escape from handcuffs. He later joined the Governor, but betrayed him to help his brother and the group. He was shot by the Governor and turned into a walker. Daryl found him and killed him in tears.
  • Sasha Williams (Sonequa Martin-Green): A former firefighter and Abraham’s girlfriend, Sasha was a fierce and brave fighter who suffered many losses in the apocalypse. She volunteered to be part of a plan to assassinate Negan, but was captured by him. She took a suicide pill and turned into a walker, hoping to kill Negan when he opened her coffin. However, he was prepared and avoided her attack. Maggie and Jesus put her down and buried her.
  • Tyreese Williams (Chad L. Coleman): Sasha’s older brother and a former NFL player, Tyreese was a gentle giant who struggled with the violence of the world. He was bitten by a walker while saving Noah’s twin brother. He hallucinated his dead friends and enemies, who tried to convince him to either fight or give up. He died in the car on the way to the hospital, with Rick, Michonne, Glenn, and Noah by his side.
  • Noah (Tyler James Williams): A young man who lived in a walled community in Virginia, Noah was rescued by Beth and Daryl from the hospital in Atlanta. He joined the group and became friends with Glenn and Tyreese. He was killed by a horde of walkers in a revolving door, while Nicholas, a cowardly survivor from Alexandria, panicked and fled. Glenn witnessed his death and was traumatized by it.
  • Denise Cloyd (Merritt Wever): A doctor and Tara’s girlfriend, Denise was a timid and insecure woman who wanted to help others. She was killed by Dwight, one of Negan’s men, who shot her in the eye with Daryl’s crossbow. She died in front of Daryl and Rosita, who had taken her on a supply run.
  • Spencer Monroe (Austin Nichols): The son of Deanna, the leader of Alexandria, Spencer was a spoiled and cowardly man who resented Rick and his group. He tried to make a deal with Negan behind Rick’s back, but Negan saw through his lies and gutted him in front of everyone. He then reanimated as a walker and was stabbed by Rick.
  • Nicholas (Michael Traynor): A survivor from Alexandria and Glenn’s nemesis, Nicholas was a selfish and cowardly man who caused the deaths of many of his friends. He tried to kill Glenn twice, but Glenn spared him and tried to redeem him. He committed suicide by shooting himself in the head, causing Glenn to fall into a pile of walkers.
  • Jessie Anderson (Alexandra Breckenridge): A former housewife and Rick’s love interest, Jessie was abused by her husband Pete, who Rick killed. She and her sons, Ron and Sam, joined Rick’s group and moved into his house. She was killed by a horde of walkers during an escape attempt, along with Sam and Ron. Rick had to chop off her hand to free Carl, who she was holding onto.
  • Enid (Katelyn Nacon): A teenage girl and Carl’s girlfriend, Enid was a survivor from Alexandria who had a tragic past. She lost her parents to walkers and had to fend for herself for a long time. She was loyal to Carl and the group, and became a medic under Siddiq. She was killed by Alpha, the leader of the Whisperers, who decapitated her and put her head on a pike.
  • Jesus (Tom Payne): A scout and a fighter from the Hilltop, Jesus was a skilled and charismatic man who befriended Rick’s group and introduced them to other communities. He was the right-hand man of Maggie, the leader of the Hilltop, and later became the leader himself. He was killed by a Whisperer, who stabbed him in the back while pretending to be a walker.
  • Tara Chambler (Alanna Masterson): A former police academy student and Denise’s girlfriend, Tara was a cheerful and loyal member of the group. She was one of the few survivors of the Governor’s attack on the prison, and was taken in by Glenn. She became the leader of the Oceanside, a community of women who had lost their men to the Saviors. She was killed by Alpha, who decapitated her and put her head on a pike.
  • Henry (Matt Lintz): The son of Ezekiel and Carol, Henry was a brave and naive boy who wanted to be a hero. He followed Carol and Morgan to the Kingdom, where he learned how to fight with a stick. He developed a crush on Lydia, a Whisperer who was captured by Daryl. He helped her escape and joined her in the Whisperers’ camp, but was betrayed by her mother, Alpha. He was killed by Alpha, who decapitated him and put his head on a pike.
